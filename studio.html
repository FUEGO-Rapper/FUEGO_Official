🎧 Studio Setup

Great music doesn't always come from expensive studios. Sometimes it starts with passion, creativity, and making the most of what you have.

Recording Setup

My primary microphone is the K669B USB microphone. It gives me a simple and reliable way to record vocals while continuing to improve my sound and technique.

Software

I use BandLab for recording, writing, and publishing music. BandLab allows me to create tracks from anywhere and focus on developing my skills as an artist.

I also use Soundtrap for production and experimenting with ideas. Between BandLab and Soundtrap, I can write, record, arrange, and build songs from start to finish.

The Studio

My studio isn't a giant professional building. Most of my recording happens in the garage where I can work with fewer interruptions and less background noise.

Having a quiet space helps me focus on recording vocals, writing lyrics, and bringing new ideas to life.

Production Style

My music blends Christian rap with modern trap influences. I enjoy energetic beats, powerful drums, strong 808s, memorable hooks, and lyrics that point people toward God.

Whether it's a worship-focused song like NO GREATER LOVE or a high-energy track like BOOM, every song begins with a purpose and a message.

"You don't need a million-dollar studio to start. You just need a reason to create."

— FUEGO
